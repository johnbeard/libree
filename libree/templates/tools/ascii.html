ASCII is one of the simplest forms of text encoding used. Every character is represented by a single byte, of which only 7 bits are used, giving 127 possible codepoints. "Printable" characters are those from 20 (space) to 126, the other characters are "non-printing" and include things like new lines, system beep, null characters and so on.
